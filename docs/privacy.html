Privacy Policy

Last updated: 23 March 2026

DiabetesPredict UK ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Data Controller

The data controller is DiabetesPredict UK. For enquiries, contact us at privacy@diabetespredict.co.uk.

2. What Data We Collect

  • Health metrics you submit: The numeric feature values (F1–F34 for glucose, F1–F20 for diabetes) entered into our prediction forms. These are processed in real time and not stored after the prediction is returned.
  • Account data (Pro users): Email address, payment information (processed by Stripe — we do not store card details).
  • Usage data: IP address (for rate limiting only, not stored long-term), pages visited, timestamps.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): You explicitly submit health data for prediction. You can withdraw consent at any time by stopping use of the service.
  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): Processing necessary to provide the prediction service you requested.
  • Legitimate Interest (Article 6(1)(f)): Rate limiting and abuse prevention.

4. Special Category Data

The health metrics you submit may constitute special category data under Article 9 of UK GDPR. We process this data based on your explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a)), given when you submit the prediction form. This data is processed in memory only and is not persisted to any database or log file.

5. Data Retention

  • Prediction inputs: Not retained. Processed in memory and discarded immediately after returning the result.
  • Account data: Retained for the duration of your Pro subscription plus 6 years (HMRC requirements).
  • Rate limiting data: IP-based counters reset daily. No long-term storage.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell your data. We share data only with:

  • Stripe: Payment processing (Pro subscriptions). See Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Hosting provider: Our infrastructure provider processes data as a data processor under a Data Processing Agreement.

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Rectify inaccurate data
  • Erase your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restrict processing
  • Data portability
  • Object to processing
  • Withdraw consent at any time

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@diabetespredict.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Cookies

We use only essential cookies (cookie consent preference). We do not use advertising, analytics, or tracking cookies. See our cookie banner for controls.

9. International Transfers

Your data is processed within the UK and EEA. If any data is transferred outside these regions, we ensure adequate safeguards are in place (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses).

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures including encryption in transit (TLS), access controls, and regular security reviews.

11. Complaints

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint.

12. Changes to This Policy

We will notify users of material changes via the website. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.
